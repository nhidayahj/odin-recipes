Malaysian Nasi Lemak

Photo by Alpha from Flickr

Description

A staple Malaysian dish of coconut rice, side of hot chilli paste (sambal), topped with crispy anchovies and fried peanuts, cucumbers, egg. A dish that can be eaten any time of day for any occasion.

Ingredients

Rice

  • 2 cups of coconut milk
  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 cups of long grain rice, rinsed and drained
  • 1 (1/2 inch) fresh ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 whole bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

Garnish

  • 1 cup of oil for frying
  • 1 cup of raw peanuts
  • 1 cup of achovies, washed
  • 1 medium cucumber, sliced

Sauce

  • 2 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 3 medium shallots, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbs chilli paste
  • 1 (4 ounce) package white achovies, washed
  • 1/4 cup of tamarind juice
  • 3 tbs of white sugar
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Make rice. Stir coconut milk, water, rice, fresh ginger, ground ginger, bay leaf and salt together in saucepan.
  2. Cover and bring to a boil over medium heat. Reduce heat and simmer until tender. Discard bay leaf and keep rice warm before serving.
  3. Make the garnish. Heat 1 cup of vegetable oil in large skillet over medium-high heat. Stir in peanuts and cook briefly, until lightly browned. Remove peanuts onto paper towels.
  4. In the same oil, fry 1 cup of achovies until crips, and remove onto paper towel.
  5. Make the sauce. Heat oil in clean skillet. Stir in onion, shallots, and garlic; cook until fragrant about 2 minutes.
  6. Mix in chilli paste and cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally; if mixture is dry, add in water as needed.
  7. Stir in remaining achovies and cook for 5 minutees. Stir in tamarind juice, sugar, salt; simmer until sauce is thick.
  8. Prepare plate with warm rice, sauce and topped with fried anchovies, peanuts, sauce, hard-boiled eggs and sliced cucumber. Serve immediately.
